Some tips to keep you safe

while your visit in Rio:

Dont's

  • leave your bag unattended.
  • put your wallet in your back pocket or the outside pocket of a bag.
  • walk alone on the beach late at night.
  • take out and/or use your mobile more than absolutely necessary on the streets
  • take more than you need to the beach or for walking.
  • open or carry a camera.
  • flash your valuables.
  • wear “gringo clothes” (call atention).
  • wander into favelas.
  • drink sink water.
  • take street cabs.
  • accept any ride.
  • ask for any kind of information on the streets.
  • take buses.

Do

  • wait for hotel taxi or Uber Black inside Fairmont or any restaurant.
  • put your money, credit cards, passport and personal belongins into the hotel safety.
  • carry only a coloured copy of your Passaport with you.
  • only request UBER BLACK
  • roll up the windows of your car/ taxi / uber black when in a transfer.
  • ask information at safe places such as hotel before leaving for an a private activity.
  • inform flight schedules if there are any changes.

RECOMENDATIONS:

  • 1. Plan ahead:

    Reach out to our local team on the ground with any questions or advice.

  • 2. Stay safe:

    Be aware of your surroundings and avoid displaying valuables or carrying large sums of cash.

    Keep an eye on your belongings at all times, particularly on beaches and in crowded areas.

  • 3. Security:

    Please contact our team in Brazil if you plan to do any external activities.

    Keep ir personal belongins, Passaport and credit card and cash on Fairmont safety.
    Just carry a copy of your passaport with you.

  • 4. Dress comfortably:

    Rio de Janeiro is known for its tropical climate, so pack light and breathable clothing, comfortable shoes, and a hat for sun protection. Keep in mind that June is Winter season in Brazil and sometimes it rains, so the temperatures can drop to 22C/18C (71F/64)

  • 5. Discover local cuisine:

    Try traditional Brazilian dishes like “feijoada” (black beans) or “churrasco”, and don't miss out on also eating some "açaí" (traditional fruit from Amazonas) and "pão de queijo" (cheese ball).

  • 6. Visit popular attractions:

    Some must-see places in Rio include Sugarloaf Mountain (we have you covered), Christ the Redeemer statue, Copacabana and Ipanema beaches and the Lapa Arches. As Lapa is a downtown and not safe area, please ask for a local guide to be with you.

  • 7. Respect culture and customs:

    Be respectful of local customs and traditions, and dress modestly when visiting churches or religious sites.
